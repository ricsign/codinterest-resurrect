Fruits Decomposition

Problem Description

A rotten fruit will decompose in the soil, and provides the soil nutritions. Therefore other plants will have better growth. Every fruit has a nutrition value of n, where n is a positive integer. The process of decomposition yields to the following:

1- A fruit piece has nutrition value of n will decompose into two smaller fruit pieces having nutrition values of m and n/m, where m is n’s smallest prime factor with m is strictly less than n. So now there’re 2 fruit pieces after decomposition.

2- Keep decomposing the fruit pieces as many as possible, until no more fruit piece can be decomposed into smaller pieces.

Return the sum of nutrition value of all final fruit pieces that cannot be further decomposed.

(*Note: You can always re-decompose a decomposed fruit piece, as long as it meets the decomposition requirements)

Example

Input 1: n = 12
Output 1: 7
Explanation 1: Initially, the fruit has nutrition value of 12, it can decompose into pieces of 2 and 6, for the first piece, it cannot be decomposed again, but for the second piece, it can be further decomposed into pieces of 2 and 3, note both of these two pieces are not possible to re-decompose, so the final fruit pieces we cannot decompose further are with nutrition value of 2,2,3, and 2+2+3 = 7, therefore yield 7.
Input 2: n = 32
Output 2: 10
